I am Yan Xu, a third-year PhD student at MIT EECS, advised by Professor [Ruonan Han](https://hangroup.mit.edu/). Before joining MIT, I completed my undergraduate studies at the [University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC)](https://en.uestc.edu.cn/) from 2019 to 2023. I was fortunate to work with Professor [Cheng Wang](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=syt2VOwAAAAJ&hl=en) on high precision and high speed DAC design. I was also grateful for the opportunity to visit Rice University in 2022, where I worked in Professor [Taiyun Chi](https://chilab.info/)’s group on transmitter design for brain-computer interfaces, with the wonderful mentorship of [Yingying Fan](https://engineering.washu.edu/faculty/Yingying-Fan.html), who is now an Assistant Professor at Washington University in St. Louis. In 2024, I interned with the Apple Hardware team, working on wireless integrated circuit design.

As a PhD student, my research interests have gradually shifted toward Generative AI, especially language models and agentic AI for mixed-signal IC design. I am broadly interested in agentic reinforcement learning (RL), circuit representation, and AI-assisted circuit design. Please feel free to reach out if you are interested in connecting or collaborating.

Outside research, I enjoy [lead climbing, bouldering, mountaineering, hiking, skiing, and running](/miscellaneous/).
